This is how many years of current earnings you're paying for when you buy one share.

IndiaMART earned ₹91.7 per share last year (EPS). The stock costs ₹1,970. So you're paying 1,970 ÷ 91.7 = 20× one year's earnings.

Gross Margin
How much of each rupee of revenue survives as profit

Think of margins as a waterfall — each stage takes a cut, and what's left at the bottom is your profit.

For every ₹100 of revenue IndiaMART earns:

₹100  Revenue comes in
 −₹45  Direct costs
₹55  Gross Profit (55% margin)
 −₹23  Salaries, marketing, rent
₹32  Operating Profit (32%)
 +₹8   Interest on ₹3,051 Cr cash
₹40  Net Profit (40% margin)

Wait — net margin (40%) is HIGHER than operating margin (32%)? Yes. IndiaMART earns ~₹135 Cr/quarter in interest on its massive cash pile. The cash itself is generating profit.

ROCE of 34% means every ₹100 of capital generates ₹34 of operating profit. Above 20% is excellent. Above 30% is rare.

Paying Suppliers
−1,000 this quarter · First decline ever

This is the #1 debate about IndiaMART — and the main reason the stock is cheap.

IndiaMART makes money by charging suppliers an annual subscription (₹30K–₹11L/year) to be listed. Revenue = Subscribers × ARPU.

221,000 businesses currently pay. That number dropped by 1,000 this quarter — the first time it's ever gone negative. For 6+ quarters, net additions have been stuck at +1K to +3K.

Why? Silver-tier subscribers (~₹30K/year) are churning at ~7% per month — roughly half of new silver customers leave within a year. Management has been trying to fix this for 6 quarters with "no significant improvement."

Bull: Only 1.5% of India's 15–20M GST-registered suppliers are paying. Huge TAM.

Bear: If the addressable market willing to pay ₹30K+/year is smaller than 15M, 220K may be the ceiling.

Key Themes — 6 Quarter Analysis

The dominant narratives emerging from 6 quarters of concall transcripts, investor decks, and financial results.

1. Subscriber Growth Stall

Paying suppliers peaked at 222K (Q2 FY26) and declined to 221K in Q3 — the first negative quarter. Net additions have been +1K to +3K for several quarters. Silver churn at ~7% monthly remains elevated despite 4-5 quarters of initiatives. Management explicitly refused to give subscriber guidance.

2. ARPU as the Growth Engine

With subscribers stuck, revenue growth is entirely ARPU-driven: ₹62K → ₹64K → ₹65K → ₹67K over 4 quarters (6-7% YoY). Top 1% pay ₹11 lakh/year (17% of revenue), top 10% pay ₹3.3 lakh (50% of revenue). Management says 6-8% ARPU CAGR is sustainable long-term.

3. Busy Infotech Inflection

Accounting software subsidiary growing 28-57% YoY in billing, deferred revenue +56-63%, 12K new licenses/quarter. Revenue from ₹66 Cr (FY25) to implied ₹110+ Cr annual run rate. Still only ₹5 Cr EBITDA/quarter but ₹112 Cr deferred revenue signals accelerating revenue recognition ahead.

4. AI: Both Threat and Opportunity

CEO candidly acknowledged AI impact. Threat: crawlers polluting traffic so badly company is DROPPING it as a metric. Opportunity: AI voice bots doing 10-20K calls/day (target 1 lakh/day), RFQ enrichment (80-90% now with quantity+spec vs 50-60% earlier). Behavioral data moat believed defensible.

5. Margin Compression (Intentional)

EBITDA margin from 38% to 33%. Drivers: new ESOP grants (₹90 Cr total), performance marketing (₹6 Cr/quarter), headcount +6-9%. CEO himself guided 33-35% as sustainable — 38%+ was artificially elevated.

6. Platform Quality Improvements

RFQ quality from 50-60% to 80-90% with quantity+specifications. Suppliers per buyer reduced from 6-7 to 4-5 (creating more competition, improving responsiveness to 70-80%). Localization from all-India to city-preferred. WhatsApp integration adding 1-2% to enquiries.

7. Vertical Strategy Emerging

CEO acknowledged need to think about "verticals within horizontal" — cultivating specific industries (pharma, engineering, packaging) more deeply. Started hiring industry-specific people. Major strategic shift from "one platform fits all" to sector-specific optimization.

8. Customer Segmentation Insight

CEO described four supplier types: (1) digitally savvy early adopters, (2) new businesses with <1yr GST who naturally churn high, (3) serious businesses with dedicated IndiaMART staff, (4) traditional offline businesses — the "fat middle" where the platform is "too complex for the new genuine customer." This gap is the fix target.

9. Export Opportunity — Small but Real

IndiaMART's "Verified Exporter" program generates ₹50-100 Cr revenue. CEO doesn't see it becoming ₹500-1,500 Cr — export market TAM has been single-digit CAGR for 10-15 years. A nice addition, not a growth driver.

Investment Thesis

The case for and against — with evidence from 6 quarters of concalls, 5 years of financials, and 11 years of efficiency data.

Bull Case

  • P/E at all-time low (19.6× vs median 48.9×) while earnings grew 55% — extreme value if any re-rating happens
  • Net cash ₹2,868 Cr = 24% of MCap. Strip out cash, you're paying ₹8,969 Cr for a business generating ₹675 Cr cash annually — 7.5% FCF yield
  • Busy Infotech growing 50%+ with ₹112 Cr deferred revenue — could become ₹200+ Cr revenue in 2 years. IndiaMART's 220K paying SME customers are a captive distribution channel for accounting software
  • ROCE 34%, negative working capital, zero debt — capital-light subscription model
  • CEO credibility rare among Indian mid-caps — under-promises, over-delivers

Bear Case

  • Subscriber growth structurally stalled at 220K — ARPU alone caps revenue at 6-8% growth
  • Silver churn 7% monthly, no improvement in 6+ quarters. Pipeline to gold/platinum may dry up
  • AI makes catalogues commodity — competitors can replicate product listings
  • Other income ₹10–135 Cr/quarter swings make NI unpredictable. On normalized operating earnings (excluding treasury), the P/E is closer to 25× — not as cheap as the headline 19.6× suggests
  • CEO succession risk — Dinesh Agarwal (56) with 49% stake, no obvious second

What to Watch

  • Q4 FY26 subscriber adds — seasonally strong, if negative again confirms structural stall
  • Silver churn trend — even 1ppt improvement adds ~2K net subs/quarter
  • Busy Infotech license acceleration — 12K/quarter sustaining?
  • AI voice bot scale: 10-20K → 1 lakh calls/day
  • Post-FY26 AGM capital return — buyback at these valuations highly accretive
  • Vertical strategy — first results from category optimization
