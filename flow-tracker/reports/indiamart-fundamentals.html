How to Read This Report

P/E RatioPrice ÷ Earnings Per Share. If a stock earns ₹100/share and trades at ₹2,000, P/E = 20x. You're paying ₹20 for every ₹1 of annual profit. Lower = cheaper. But compare to its own history, not other companies — a tech platform will always trade at higher P/E than a bank.
PEG RatioP/E ÷ Earnings Growth %. PEG < 1 means you're paying less for growth than it's delivering. PEG 0.36 means this stock's P/E (20x) is very low relative to its growth (55%). Below 1 = undervalued for growth.
EV/EBITDAEnterprise Value ÷ Operating Profit (before depreciation, interest, tax). Removes the effect of debt/cash. Better than P/E for comparing companies with different capital structures. Lower = cheaper.
P/Sales, EV/RevenueHow much you pay per rupee of revenue. Useful for platform/SaaS companies where margins are still evolving. Lower = cheaper.
ROCEReturn on Capital Employed. How much profit the business generates per ₹1 invested in it. >15% = good business, >20% = excellent. Higher = better.
ROEReturn on Equity. Profit as % of shareholder money. Very high ROE (>40%) often means asset-light business with little equity needed. Higher = better.
D/E RatioTotal Debt ÷ Total Equity. How leveraged the company is. <1 = comfortable, >2 = heavily indebted, near 0 = no debt (best). Lower = safer.
CFO/NICash From Operations ÷ Net Income. Are profits real (backed by cash) or just accounting? >1.0x means the company collects more cash than it reports as profit. Above 1 = real profits.
Operating MarginOperating Profit ÷ Revenue. What % of revenue becomes profit after running costs. 30% = for every ₹100 earned, ₹30 is profit. Higher = more profitable.
BetaHow much the stock moves vs the market (Nifty). Beta 1.0 = moves same as Nifty. Beta 0.2 = moves only 20% as much. Low beta = defensive, high beta = volatile.
Book Value/ShareTotal equity (assets minus liabilities) ÷ shares. The "accounting value" of each share. If stock price < BVPS, you're buying assets at a discount.
Delivery %What % of daily trading volume was actually taken delivery of (transferred to demat accounts). High delivery (>60%) = people buying to hold. Low (<30%) = intraday speculation.

Quarterly Revenue, Net Income & Operating Margin

Source: Screener.in (Indian GAAP standalone). Revenue & Net Income in ₹Cr (bars, left axis). Operating Margin % (orange line, right axis).

How to read: Blue bars = total revenue (should be growing). Green bars = net profit (what shareholders keep). Orange line = operating margin (what % of revenue becomes operating profit). What to look for: (1) Revenue bars getting taller each quarter = business growing. (2) Green bars growing faster than blue = margin expansion. (3) Orange line stable or rising = business becoming more efficient. Here you'll see margins were artificially high during COVID (costs dropped) then normalized to 25-35%, which is the sustainable range.
