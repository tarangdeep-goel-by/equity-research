Financial Terms Used in This Report — Plain English Guide

Every term you'll see below, explained with INDIAMART's actual numbers as examples.

CMPCurrent Market Price — what one share costs right now on the stock exchange. INDIAMART's CMP is ₹1,970. This changes every second during market hours.
Market CapMarket Capitalization — the total value of ALL shares of the company. CMP × Total Shares = ₹1,970 × 6.0 Cr shares = ₹11,837 Cr. Think of it as "how much would it cost to buy the entire company?"
EPSEarnings Per Share — how much profit the company made for each share. Net Profit / Total Shares. INDIAMART earned ₹551 Cr in the last FY with 6.0 Cr shares, so EPS = ~₹91.7/share.
P/E RatioPrice-to-Earnings — how many years of current earnings you're paying for. CMP / EPS. INDIAMART: ₹1,970 / ₹91.7 = 19.6x. Lower P/E = cheaper.
ATLAll-Time Low — the lowest value this metric has ever reached in the stock's history.
EV/EBITDAEnterprise Value / EBITDA. EV = Market Cap + Debt - Cash. Better than P/E because it adjusts for cash/debt on the balance sheet.
EBITDAEarnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortisation — how much cash the core business generates before accounting items.
Gross Margin% of revenue left after direct costs. Higher = better pricing power.
OPMOperating Profit Margin — % of revenue left after ALL operating costs. Best measure of core business profitability.
Net MarginFinal % left after everything — costs, interest, taxes, other income.
ROCEReturn on Capital Employed — Operating Profit / Capital Employed. Above 20% is excellent. INDIAMART's 34.0% means every ₹100 of capital generates ₹34.0 of operating profit.
D/E RatioDebt-to-Equity — how leveraged the company is. INDIAMART's 0.011 means virtually zero debt. Below 0.5 = conservative, above 1 = aggressive.
Current RatioCurrent Assets / Current Liabilities. Above 1.5 = comfortable. INDIAMART: 2.39x.
OCF / CFOOperating Cash Flow — actual cash generated by the business. When OCF > Net Profit, earnings are "real."
BetaStock's sensitivity to market moves. INDIAMART's 0.20 means if Nifty falls 10%, INDIAMART typically falls only 2%.
Deferred RevenueCash collected but not yet recognized as revenue. High growth in deferred revenue = future revenue already locked in.
BVPSBook Value Per Share — net assets / shares. INDIAMART: ₹390. Stock at ₹1,970 = 5.1x book value.
Working Capital DaysHow many days of revenue are tied up in operations. Negative = customers fund the business (subscription model).
