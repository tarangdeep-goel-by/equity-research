Valuation — Is it cheap?

The single most important question for any investor: what are you paying relative to what you're getting?

This stock is at its cheapest-ever valuation

Trailing P/E of 19.6× vs 6-year median of 48.9× — the 0th percentile. In 323 weekly observations since IPO, IndiaMART has never been this cheap relative to its earnings. The stock is down 30% from its 52-week high (₹2,799) while earnings grew 55%.

What is P/E? Price-to-Earnings ratio tells you how many years of current earnings you're paying for. IndiaMART's EPS (Earnings Per Share) is ₹92. At ₹1,970 per share, you're paying 1,970 ÷ 92 = 21.4 years of earnings. The lower this number, the "cheaper" the stock. IndiaMART's P/E of 19.6× is the cheapest it has ever been — for comparison, its median P/E is 48.9× (meaning investors have historically been willing to pay 49 years of earnings for this stock).

Why negative working capital days matter: Most companies have positive working capital days (they ship goods, then wait 60-90 days to get paid). IndiaMART's −305 days means the opposite: customers pay annual subscriptions upfront before the company delivers any service. Investment Thesis

The case for and against this stock, and what would change each view.

Bull Case

  • P/E at all-time low (19.6× vs median 48.9×) while earnings grew 55% — extreme value if any re-rating happens
  • Net cash ₹2,868 Cr = 24% of market cap. Strip out cash and you're paying ₹8,969 Cr for a business generating ₹675 Cr cash annually — 7.5% FCF yield
  • Busy Infotech growing 50%+ with ₹112 Cr deferred revenue — could become ₹200+ Cr revenue in 2 years
  • ROCE 34%, negative working capital, zero debt — capital-light cash machine with subscription economics
  • Management credibility is rare — candid CEO who under-promises and over-delivers

Bear Case

  • Subscriber growth structurally stalled at 220K — ARPU alone may cap revenue growth at 6-8% per year forever
  • Silver churn at 7% monthly with no improvement after 6+ quarters. If the funnel dries up, gold/platinum eventually erodes
  • AI makes catalogues commodity — what's left if competitors can replicate product listings easily?
  • Other income volatility makes quarterly NI unpredictable. Operating P/E (excluding treasury) is closer to 25×
  • CEO is 56 with 49% stake — succession risk. No obvious second-in-command for core IndiaMART strategy

What to Watch

  • Q4 FY26 subscriber adds — Q4 is seasonally strong. If negative again, confirms structural stall
  • Silver churn trend — even 1ppt improvement adds ~2K net subscribers per quarter
  • Busy Infotech license growth — 12K/quarter sustaining? Revenue recognition acceleration?
  • AI voice bot scale-up — 10-20K to 1 lakh calls/day. Impact on retention costs?
  • Post-FY26 AGM capital return — ₹200-300 Cr buyback at these valuations would be highly accretive
  • Vertical strategy — first results from industry-specific hiring and category optimization
Financial Terms Glossary — Every Term Used in This Report Explained
CMPCurrent Market Price — what one share costs on the stock exchange right now. IndiaMART: ₹1,970.
Market CapMarket Capitalization — total value of ALL shares. Price × Shares = ₹1,970 × 6 Cr = ₹11,837 Cr. "How much to buy the entire company."
EPSEarnings Per Share — Net Profit / Total Shares. ₹551 Cr / 6 Cr shares = ₹92. "What the company earned for each share you own."
P/E RatioPrice-to-Earnings — CMP / EPS = ₹1,970 / ₹92 = 19.6×. "How many years of current earnings you're paying for." Lower = cheaper.
EV/EBITDAEnterprise Value / Operating profit. EV = Market Cap + Debt − Cash. Better than P/E because it adjusts for how much cash/debt the company has.
ROCEReturn on Capital Employed — Operating Profit / Capital Employed. IndiaMART's 34% means every ₹100 invested generates ₹34 of operating profit. Above 20% is excellent.
D/EDebt-to-Equity — how leveraged the company is. IndiaMART's 0.011 = virtually zero debt. Below 0.5 = conservative.
OCF / CFOOperating Cash Flow — actual cash generated by operations. When OCF > Net Profit, earnings are "real" (backed by cash, not accounting tricks).
BetaStock's sensitivity to market moves. 0.20 = if Nifty falls 10%, IndiaMART typically falls only 2%.
ARPUAverage Revenue Per User — how much each paying customer pays per year. IndiaMART: ₹67K. The top 1% pay ₹11 lakh/year.
Deferred RevenueCash collected but not yet "earned." When a customer pays ₹60K for a year, only ₹5K is revenue each month. The rest (₹55K) sits as deferred revenue — guaranteed future revenue.
Working Capital DaysHow many days of revenue are tied up in operations. Negative = customers fund the business (subscription model). IndiaMART: −305 days.
