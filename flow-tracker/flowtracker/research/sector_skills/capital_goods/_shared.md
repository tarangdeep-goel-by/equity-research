## Capital Goods / Industrials Mode (Auto-Detected)

This is a capital goods, engineering, or industrial company. Order book visibility and execution drive valuations.

**Primary Metrics:**
- **Order Book / Order Inflow** — the forward revenue pipeline. Track Book-to-Bill ratio (order inflow / revenue). >1.0x = growing pipeline
- **Order Book / Revenue ratio** — execution visibility in years. 2-3x = strong, <1x = weak pipeline
- **Execution Run-Rate** — quarterly revenue as % of opening order book. Measures execution speed
- **Working Capital Cycle** — capital goods have long WC cycles (advance-heavy, receivable-heavy). Track CCC direction

**Revenue Quality:**
- **Fixed-price vs cost-plus contracts** — fixed-price = margin risk on input cost spikes; cost-plus = margin protection
- **Domestic vs export mix** — export orders often have better margins but FX risk
- **Government vs private orders** — government orders = longer payment cycles but steady flow

**Valuation:** PE is primary. EV/EBITDA acceptable. DCF valid if order book provides cash flow visibility. Premium justified for companies with strong order book growth + improving margins.

**Emphasize:** Order inflow trajectory, book-to-bill, execution timelines, margin profile of new vs legacy orders, counterparty quality (government/PSU/private), and FX impact on export orders.
