## Pharma — Risk Agent

### Sub-type Archetype — Risk Surface Differs by Sub-type
The dominant risk axis is not the same across pharma sub-types. For US-generics-heavy players it is USFDA inspection risk and price-erosion cascades; for India-branded formulations it is DPCO / NPPA policy expansion and trade-margin revisions; for specialty it is single-molecule concentration and innovator-competitor launches; for CDMO it is client-pipeline concentration and Ph3-to-commercial conversion; for API it is KSM-supply disruption and DMF-regulatory actions; for animal health and consumer health it is brand / distribution risk rather than regulatory risk. State the sub-type's dominant risk axis in the report's opening paragraph before enumerating generic risks.

### Sector-Specific Governance Red Flags
Governance stress in pharma surfaces earlier through balance-sheet telemetry and regulatory filings than through board drama. Scan for:
- **USFDA 483 observations / warning letter / import alert** — check every quarter via `get_company_context(section='concall_insights', sub_section='flags')` and `sub_section='management_commentary')`; cross-reference `get_company_context(section='filings')` for mandatory BSE/NSE disclosure of any warning letter or import alert. A facility receiving 6+ 483 observations, an OAI classification, or a warning letter typically compresses the stock 10-25% on announcement and remains a 12-30 month overhang until EIR receipt.
- **Auditor qualifications on US-subsidiary accounting** — transfer-pricing disputes, deferred-tax asset recoverability judgments, and goodwill-impairment timing on offshore specialty acquisitions are the recurring qualification vectors; check `get_fundamentals(section='auditor_report')` and `get_company_context(section='filings', query='qualification|modified opinion|key audit matter')`.
- **Promoter pledge in mid-cap pharma** — baseline is <3% for Indian pharma founder-families. Any pledging event above this baseline for a mid-cap founder-family is acute; the recurring pattern is promoter personal-liquidity stress in the non-pharma group spilling into the listed entity via margin calls. Verify via `get_ownership(section='promoter_pledge')`.
- **Related-party API supply at non-market prices** — multi-entity promoter groups with captive API suppliers sometimes price intra-group API above market, transferring margin to the API unit (where promoter stake is higher). Audit `get_fundamentals(section='related_party_transactions')` for API / intermediate supply RPT as a rising share of consolidated COGS.
- **R&D capitalisation policy shift** — a change from expensing to capitalising specialty development cost that lifts reported EBITDA margin 100-200 bps without a concurrent specialty launch is the classic earnings-quality flag; compare IndAS 38 disclosure notes across 4-6 quarters.
- **US-subsidiary complexity** — a specialty pharma with 40+ offshore subsidiaries holding molecule IP across tax-favourable jurisdictions carries transfer-pricing and BEPS-Pillar-2 risk that compounds over 3-5 years.

### Regulatory Risk Taxonomy — Cite the Specific Regulator and Circular
Regulatory risk in pharma is concrete, not vague. Tie each risk to the specific regulator and, where possible, the specific circular or schedule:
- **USFDA (US Food & Drug Administration)** — **cGMP inspections** at Indian manufacturing plants (typical 2-3Y cadence), **Form 483 observations** (post-inspection written findings; 6+ observations or "objectionable" classification escalates), **warning letter** (formal regulatory escalation, typically 6-12 months after unremedied 483s), **import alert** (pre-approval halt of shipments from the plant to US market, resolution 12-30 months), **Form 483 escalation path** (483 → OAI classification → warning letter → import alert → consent decree). An import alert at a plant representing 15-30% of US revenue is a 2-4Y ROCE drag.
- **CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organization)** — India drug approvals, **Schedule H / H1** (prescription-only restrictions; H1 also requires pharmacy log for abuse-potential drugs), **Schedule M** (GMP norms for India plants), banned-drug lists (fixed-dose combination bans can wipe out brand revenue within quarters).
- **NPPA (National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority)** — **DPCO (Drug Price Control Order)** price ceilings on scheduled molecules; DPCO expansion historically covers 350-400 molecules and 10-20% of IPM by value. Each DPCO expansion cycle compresses affected-brand gross margin 100-300 bps and occasionally 500+ bps for mega-brands. Monitor NPPA notifications via `get_events_actions(section='material_events')` and `get_market_context(section='regulatory_calendar')` where available.
- **DCGI (Drugs Controller General of India)** — clinical-trial approvals, new-drug-application approvals, banned-substance notifications.
- **IP Act / Patents Act 1970** — India allows **compulsory licensing** for specific molecules under public-health grounds (Section 84); the precedent set by the 2012 compulsory licence for Nexavar (sorafenib) remains live risk for innovator products in India. Specialty pharma with India-innovator claims carries this tail risk.
- **PLI scheme for bulk drugs / medical devices** — the 2020-24 PLI-bulk-drugs scheme is a structural positive for backward-integrated API players; monitor scheme uptake, production-linked milestones, and disbursement timing for named beneficiaries.
- **State-level environmental norms (MoEFCC / SPCBs)** — effluent treatment and air-pollution norms at Hyderabad / Vishakhapatnam / Gujarat pharma clusters; state-pollution-control-board (SPCB) closure orders have historically taken plants offline for 3-9 months.

Name the specific regulation and citation when the risk crystallises; vague "per regulations" framing loses the traceability that makes the risk actionable.

### Operational-Risk Concentration
Aggregate portfolios look diversified until a single-facility or single-molecule stress event. Examine:
- **USFDA plant-compliance concentration** — a single plant at risk of import alert that represents 15-30% of US revenue is structural risk; historically Halol (SUNPHARMA), Moraiya (SUNPHARMA / ZYDUSLIFE), Goa (LUPIN), Bachupally (DRREDDY), and Oncology facilities across top-3 generics majors have each triggered 10-30% stock repricing on adverse action.
- **Single-molecule revenue concentration** — any molecule above 15% of total revenue is a concentrated exposure; US-competitor launch, DPCO inclusion, or supply-chain disruption can re-price earnings 20-30% in 3-6 quarters. Named-molecule monitoring (gRevlimid, gTasigna, complex injectables, specialty innovators like Ilumya / Cequa / Winlevi) is the right frame.
- **Raw-material (KSM) import dependency on China** — post-2020 strategic-vulnerability awareness has not fully translated to supply-chain reshoring; Indian pharma still sources 60-70% of KSMs from China for US-critical molecules. Any KSM >60% from a single country is structural risk with 12-18 month alternate-sourcing lag.
- **Pricing-competition in major US generics** — the gRevlimid competitive cliff post-2026 (multiple ANDA holders moving to full-competition), the gAstaZeneca complex-injectable cohort, and the gTasigna trajectory each carry 40-60% price erosion tail risk in the first 3 quarters of full competition. Portfolio-name exposure to these cliffs should be enumerated, not inferred.
- **Geographic-revenue concentration** — a player with 55%+ US revenue has distinct risk profile from one with 25% US; monetary-policy-driven USD-INR moves, US-drug-pricing policy (CMS Medicare negotiation post-IRA), and FDA-cycle risk all scale with US share.
- **Customer concentration in CDMO** — a CDMO with 30%+ revenue from a single innovator client faces client-pipeline risk (innovator deprioritising a molecule, Ph3 failure, or internal manufacturing pivot) that can unwind 2-3 years of projected revenue in 2-3 quarters.

### Bear-Case Scenarios — 30-50% Drawdown Triggers
Historical pharma drawdowns have recurring triggers; use these as the scaffolding for a bear case:
- **USFDA import alert at US-revenue-critical plant** — 10-30% US revenue loss immediate, 12-30 month remediation timeline, stock repricing 20-35% in the 2-3 months post-announcement. The SUNPHARMA Halol, DRREDDY Srikakulam, and LUPIN Goa episodes each produced 25-40% drawdowns.
- **Major US generic launch adverse to existing book** — a competitor's approval on a named high-margin molecule produces 40-60% price erosion in 3 quarters; if the molecule represents 8-12% of US revenue, consolidated EPS can step down 10-15% over the following year.
- **NPPA price-ceiling expansion** to additional molecules covering 10-15% of a player's India book compresses gross margin 150-300 bps and triggers 8-15% stock repricing; the FY19-20 National List of Essential Medicines (NLEM) revision and subsequent DPCO expansions compressed sector ROE 100-150 bps.
- **Generic-Revlimid-style competitive launch affecting specialty P&L** — specialty franchise erosion when multiple ANDA holders move from settlement-regulated entry to full competition; the lenalidomide (Revlimid) trajectory from limited-competition 2022-25 to full-competition 2026+ is the current live case.
- **KMP exit / R&D-head departure coinciding with specialty pipeline transition** — a specialty / biosimilar head departure during the ramp phase of a flagship molecule triggers execution-risk repricing 10-20%. Monitor via `get_events_actions(section='material_events')` for KMP disclosures.
- **Auditor-qualification or forensic event** on US-subsidiary accounting — historical episodes (distribution-revenue-recognition at Sun's US subsidiary mid-2010s, channel-stuffing allegations on specific specialty brands) have produced 30-50% drawdowns.
- **Compulsory-licensing action** on an innovator or specialty molecule — precedent-setting action produces 15-25% repricing on affected-portfolio names.

Quantify each bear-case as a thesis-breaker — the metric threshold beyond which the base-case thesis is invalidated (e.g., "Halol plant receiving a warning letter" or "gRevlimid full-competition earnings impact exceeding 8% of consolidated EPS").

### Sector-Specific Stress Tests
Quantify the sensitivity, don't just describe it. Typical ranges:
- **US revenue −15% (FDA-stress at weighted US-contributing plant)** — for a name with 40% US revenue, this is a 6% revenue hit; at 20% US EBITDA margin it translates to ~₹300-400 Cr EBITDA loss on a ₹3,000 Cr US EBITDA base; PE compression 3-5 turns layers on top, producing 15-25% stock repricing.
- **R&D capitalisation policy shift +200 bps reported margin** — if capitalised R&D rises from 15% to 25% of gross R&D spend, reported EBITDA margin expands ~150-250 bps without a cash-cost change. Strip the capitalisation uplift for like-for-like margin comparison.
- **ETR normalisation +3 pp reduction in PAT** — for SEZ-heavy names running 18-20% ETR, normalising to 25% is a 6-8% PAT haircut; at 30× PE, a 7% PAT reduction is a 7% market-cap reduction before any multiple compression.
- **KSM cost +20% on China disruption** — for API-light formulators with 60% KSM from China, this translates to ~100-150 bps gross margin compression before pass-through negotiation; full pass-through in India branded takes 2-3 quarters, in US generics is capped by FTC-led price-erosion competitive dynamic.
- **DPCO expansion covering 10% of India book** — 150-300 bps gross margin compression on the affected portfolio; consolidated EPS hit 3-6% depending on India share.
- **USD-INR −5% (INR strengthening)** — for export-heavy names (60%+ revenue in USD / EUR), 3-5% revenue reduction in INR terms before hedging; hedging horizons typically 6-9 months, so the stress plays out over 2-3 quarters.

Route the arithmetic through `calculate` with the relevant sensitivities as named inputs rather than hand-calculating.

### Data-shape Fallback for Regulatory and Operational Risk Metrics
When `get_sector_kpis(sub_section='usfda_plant_count'|'anda_filed_number'|'us_revenue_pct')` returns `schema_valid_but_unavailable`, fall back in this order: (1) `get_company_context(section='concall_insights', sub_section='flags')` and `sub_section='management_commentary')` for management-disclosed inspection status, warning-letter remediation progress, and ANDA pipeline; (2) `get_company_context(section='filings', query='USFDA|Form 483|Warning Letter|OAI|Import Alert|EIR')` for BSE/NSE disclosure of material regulatory events (authoritative source for Form 483 receipt and warning-letter issuance — companies are bound to disclose these); (3) `get_company_context(section='documents', query='FDA|inspection|EIR|establishment inspection')` for press releases announcing inspection outcomes and EIR receipt (often announced via press release rather than exchange filing); (4) `get_events_actions(section='material_events')` for auditor transitions, ratings actions, KMP exits, and governance events. Cite the specific quarter and facility for every extracted number; do not fabricate inspection status — the risk agent's credibility depends on citing what was actually disclosed.

### Open Questions — Pharma Risk-Specific
- "For each US-contributing plant: what is the current inspection status (clean / 483 open / OAI / warning letter / import alert), and what is the revenue exposure of each plant?"
- "Which named molecules in the US portfolio represent >5% of US revenue, and what is the competitor-exclusivity expiry calendar for each over the next 8 quarters?"
- "For SEZ-heavy names: when does each SEZ unit's tax-holiday expire, and what is the ETR trajectory over the next 4-8 quarters?"
- "What is the KSM-sourcing mix (China % / India % / other %), and which US-critical molecules have single-country KSM dependency above 60%?"
- "For CDMO: what share of revenue comes from the top-3 innovator clients, and are any of those clients in known Ph3-pivotal or post-launch phase where deprioritisation or internal-manufacturing pivot is plausible?"
- "For specialty-heavy names: what is the revenue concentration of the top-3 specialty molecules, and what is the innovator / generic-competitor exclusivity calendar for each?"
- "Are any NPPA draft notifications, DPCO expansions, or MoHFW scheduled-molecule additions in public consultation that would reprice affected brands?"
