## Chemicals / Specialty Chemicals Mode (Auto-Detected)

This is a chemicals company. Apply chemicals-sector-specific analysis.

**Business Model:** Chemicals companies earn from converting raw materials into higher-value products. Margins are driven by raw material cycles, product mix, and value-added processing.

**Primary Metrics:**
- **Revenue mix**: Commodity chemicals (low margin, volume-driven) vs Specialty chemicals (high margin, application-driven)
- **Segment-level profitability**: Extract and analyze EBIT margins by segment (e.g., Consumer vs B2B/Industrial). Segment margin trends reveal whether growth is coming from high-quality or low-quality revenue. If segment margins aren't in structured data, check concall insights or pose as an open question
- **Gross Margin trajectory** — tracks raw material cost passthrough ability
- **Capacity utilization %** — from concall data. High utilization = pricing power + capex trigger
- **Capex commercialization**: New capacity additions and their ramp-up timelines

**Valuation:** PE and EV/EBITDA both valid. Premium for companies with:
- High specialty/value-added mix (>60% of revenue)
- Strong customer relationships (sticky B2B contracts)
- Import substitution / China+1 beneficiary positioning
- R&D pipeline for new molecules/applications
- Consumer brand moat (distribution network, brand recall, pricing power)

**Valuation consistency rule:** Your bull/bear valuation multiples MUST come from the peer group you identified via `get_yahoo_peers` or `get_peer_sector`. If the peers are consumer companies, use consumer multiples. If the peers are commodity chemicals, use commodity multiples. Do not default to generic "chemicals sector" multiples when your own analysis identifies different peers.

**Key Risks:**
- **Raw material price volatility** — crude oil, natural gas, benzene, ethylene derivatives. 1-2 quarter lag in passthrough
- **Regulatory/environmental compliance costs** — rising globally, benefits compliant Indian players
- **Customer concentration** — agrochemical/pharma intermediates often have few large customers
- **China dumping risk** — Chinese overcapacity can crater pricing in commodity chemicals

**Emphasize:** Product mix evolution, customer diversification, capex cycle (brownfield vs greenfield, expected asset turns), raw material cost trends vs selling price realization, and segment-level margin analysis.
